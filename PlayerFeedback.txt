================================================================================
DICHOTOMY - VERSION 0.1 BETA TEST PLAYER FEEDBACK
================================================================================

This document contains player feedback from the 0.1 beta test, along with 
proposed solutions for the next version.

For the original 0.1 beta rules, see OldRules.txt


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE #1: DICE IMBALANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PROBLEM:
Larger dice were strictly better than smaller dice. A D10 can still roll 1-4, 
making it superior to a D4 in all situations with no meaningful trade-off.

PROPOSED SOLUTION:
Implement a magnitude-based system where tasks require hitting specific ranges 
rather than exceeding thresholds. Success requires rolling WITHIN the target 
range (overshooting causes failure). The four magnitudes are Small (1-4), 
Medium (5-8), Large (9-12), and Huge (13-16), mirrored for both Yin (negative) 
and Yang (positive). Rolling 0 hits balanced/neutral tasks. Rolling beyond ±17 
results in critical failures.

During character creation, players assign each die a permanent bonus modifier. 
Players may choose any number, though staying between -12 and +12 is 
recommended to avoid excessive critical failures. This bonus shifts where the 
die lands on the magnitude spectrum. For example: D4+12 rolls 13-16 (100% 
Huge Yang success), D4-1 rolls 0-3 (straddling Balanced and Small Yang at 
25%/75%), D8+4 rolls 5-12 (50%/50% split between Medium and Large Yang).

This creates "focused specialist" vs "flexible generalist" tradeoffs. A D4 
can achieve 100% success in one magnitude or strategically straddle two 
magnitudes for 50%/50% reliability (acting like a D8). A D6 can favor one 
magnitude (66.7%) while touching another (33.3%). A D8 naturally straddles 
two magnitudes at 50%/50% or can spread across three. Larger dice (D10, D12) 
cover multiple magnitudes but with lower individual success rates (40%, 33.3% 
respectively), making them unreliable generalists capable of attempting any 
magnitude but less likely to succeed at any specific one.

The mental math is simple: count by 4s. Players choose whether to specialize 
(guaranteed success in narrow scenarios) or generalize (attempt anything but 
succeed less often). Each die assignment becomes a meaningful strategic 
decision about character capabilities and playstyle.

ISSUE #2: DICE HOARDING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PROBLEM:
Players felt encouraged to hoard dice rather than spend them. No mechanical 
incentive to take risks or "burn" dice on attempts.

PROPOSED SOLUTION:
Implement an experience point system that heavily rewards failure over success:

• Succeeding at a task grants 1 exp point
• Failing at a task grants 3 exp points

This creates a strong mechanical incentive for players to spend dice freely and 
take risks, even when failure is likely. The narrative success of completing a 
task is its own reward, while mechanical character advancement comes primarily 
from attempting difficult tasks and learning from failure. Players are 
encouraged to engage with challenges and drive the narrative forward rather 
than conserving resources.

Exp points are spent to progress character Threads (see Issue #3), creating a 
direct link between taking risks and advancing your character's personal story.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE #3: CHARACTER GROWTH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PROBLEM:
No good built-in character growth process. Players want meaningful progression 
and development over time. The new bonus system means dice are already optimized 
at character creation, leaving no room for mechanical stat improvement.

PROPOSED SOLUTION:
Implement Aspects and Threads as the core progression system.

ASPECTS:
Aspects are specific personal traits, items, knowledge, relationships, or 
beliefs that define your character. Examples: "Studied under Master Blacksmith 
Chen", "Carries my mother's lucky compass", "Never breaks a promise", "Knows 
the old smuggler routes". Characters start with 1 Aspect.

Before making a roll, a player may spend an applicable Aspect to gain one of 
these benefits:
  1. Invert the die's bonus for that roll (D8+10 becomes D8-10, allowing you 
     to use a die outside its normal range)
  2. Negate a critical failure, turning it into a normal failure

All spent Aspects recharge after a long rest (when all players' dice pools are 
recharged). Players must narratively justify how the specific Aspect applies to 
the situation.

THREADS:
Threads are the next story beats in your character's personal narrative - the 
questions they seek to answer, goals they pursue, or mysteries they investigate. 
Characters start with 2 Threads written down.

Players spend accumulated exp points to progress Threads in exponential 
increments: 4 exp for the first Thread, 8 exp for the second, 12 exp for the 
third, 16 exp for the fourth, and so on (continuing the pattern indefinitely). 
When you spend exp to progress a Thread, the next chapter of play incorporates 
resolution or advancement of that story beat, and you gain a new Aspect that 
emerges from that narrative progression. You then write down your next Thread, 
maintaining a running list of upcoming story goals.

This creates exponential growth: more Aspects = more flexibility and clutch 
moments = more engagement with challenges = more exp = faster Thread progression. 
Terminology: "Chapter" refers to a scene or story segment, "Volume" refers to 
a full play session.

NOTE: Thread progression costs changed to flat 20 exp per progression (see 
GMless Structure section below for reasoning).


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GENERAL CHANGE: GMLESS STRUCTURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PROPOSAL:
Shift from traditional GM-led gameplay to a GMless narrative control system 
where all players share responsibility for steering the story.

STORY POINTS:
Each player starts every Volume (play session) with 12 Story Points. These 
points are used to bid for narrative control and break ties during challenge 
magnitude voting. Story Points recharge at the start of each new Volume.

CHAPTER BIDDING:
At the start of each Chapter, all players participate in a blind bid using 
their Story Points. Each player secretly chooses how many points to bid (0 or 
more), then all bids are revealed simultaneously. The highest bidder spends 
their bid points and becomes the Chapter Director, gaining narrative control 
for that Chapter. In case of tied bids, tied players re-bid until a winner 
emerges. The Chapter Director sets the scene and guides the narrative flow 
while other players roleplay their characters and contribute.

VOLUME STRUCTURE:
Each Volume consists of 10 Chapters. Each Chapter contains exactly 4 Challenges 
that characters must attempt (though players choose which character attempts 
each challenge). This provides 40 total challenges per Volume, averaging ~10 
challenges per player in a 4-player group. At realistic success rates (~60% 
success rate), each player earns approximately 12 exp per Volume.

CHALLENGE MAGNITUDE DETERMINATION:
When a challenge arises, players must collectively determine its magnitude 
(Small, Medium, Large, or Huge). Players do NOT specify which attribute is 
required - any attribute can potentially solve the challenge as long as it hits 
the target magnitude (Yin or Yang). The process:

1. Open Vote: All players vote on the magnitude
2. Clear majority wins immediately
3. If Tied: Any tied player can spend 1 Story Point to push their choice
4. Multiple spenders: Highest magnitude among spenders wins (rewards making 
   challenges harder, which grants more exp through failure)

This allows ANY character approach to work if it narratively makes sense and 
hits the magnitude, encouraging creative problem-solving.

THREAD PROGRESSION ADJUSTMENT:
To accommodate the increased number of challenges per Volume, Thread progression 
costs are set to a flat 20 exp per progression (instead of the exponential 4, 
8, 12, 16... system). This ensures players progress approximately 1 Thread 
every 1-2 Volumes, meaning only 2-3 of the 10 Chapters per Volume focus on 
individual Thread progressions, leaving the majority of Chapters for general 
story development.
