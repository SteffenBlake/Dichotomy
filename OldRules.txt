================================================================================
DICHOTOMY - VERSION 0.1 BETA TEST RULES
================================================================================

NOTE: These are the original rules from the early 0.1 beta test, used to 
gather player feedback. For feedback on these rules, see PlayerFeedback.txt

================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Dichotomy is a dice-based Roleplaying Game meant for any number of players, 
who take on the roles of created characters in a fictional narrative of their 
own craft and desire.

One Player assumes the role of Mediator. The Mediator controls the narrative 
of all events and occurrences outside the scope of the player's characters. 
The Mediator handles making judgment calls for when a character's attempted 
action justifies rolling a dice for success. The general rule of thumb is if 
it is an action that has a reasonable chance to fail, it should require a roll 
to attempt success.

Games can be played over numerous individual Sessions, with play picking up 
where everything was left off at the end of the prior Session.

While other Roleplaying games have a more technical focus on mechanics like 
combat, character placement, tracking inventory, etc, Dichotomy is much more 
of a Narrative focused game, encouraging players to craft a story together.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CORE CONCEPT: DUALITY - YIN AND YANG
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In Dichotomy all tasks, rolls, Attributes, and bonuses are considered to be 
either Yin or Yang, with neither being "good" or "bad", just different. The 
core principle of Dichotomy is representing how nearly everything just has 
another side to its coin, and the dual nature of all things.

Yin represents the cool, calm, collected, and methodical. It is associated 
with Low and Negative numbers, rolls, and bonuses.

Yang represents the warm, energetic, emotional, and creative. It is associated 
with High and Positive numbers, rolls, and bonuses.

Everyone has a bit of both in them, and characters will naturally be created 
by players that have a balance between both Yin and Yang characteristics.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CHARACTER CREATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATTRIBUTES
----------
To begin, players assign one of each of 5 Attributes to a respective 
Polyhedral Dice that they wish to represent that Attribute. These are a D4, 
D6, D8, D10, and D12. When they wish to Perform a Task with that Attribute, 
they can "spend" the dice and roll with it, using its result as the base score 
for the roll, then add on other applicable potential Bonuses (covered in the 
"Performing Tasks" section).

The five attributes are as follows:

• ALACRITY: Represents speed and dexterity
• VIGOR: Represents strength, size, stamina, and constitution
• PULCHRITUDE: Represents beauty, creativity, intimidation, and scariness
• TEMPERAMENT: Represents willpower, focus, attitude, and flexibility
• REPUTATION: Represents popularity, recognition, who you know, and who 
  you don't know

Each attribute has both a Yin and a Yang Duality, representing the nature of 
how Attributes have divergent behaviors:

ALACRITY
  Yin (1-2):  Dexterity, Fine Motor Skills, Stealth, Craftmanship, 
              Sleight of Hand, Aiming
  Yang (7+):  Speed, Acrobatics, Reflexes, Dodging, Performing, 
              Throwing Distance

VIGOR
  Yin (1-2):  Squeezing through gaps, Sensitivity, Awareness, Insight, 
              Detection
  Yang (7+):  Strength, Might, Constitution, Resistance, Stamina

PULCHRITUDE
  Yin (1-2):  Charming, Seductive, Persuasive, Creativity, Humorous
  Yang (7+):  Terrifying, Intimidation, Obstinance, Factual, Critical

TEMPERAMENT
  Yin (1-2):  Relaxed, Level-Headed, Cool, Smooth, Fun-loving
  Yang (7+):  Focused, Competitive, Ambition, Tireless, Steadfast, Serious

REPUTATION
  Yin (1-2):  Incognito, Unrecognizeable, Blends in to a crowd, 
              Impressing Others
  Yang (7+):  (In)famous, Recognizeable, Popular, Stands out, 
              Calling in Favors

Assigning a low rolling dice (like the D4) to an attribute represents a heavy 
lean towards its Yin side, whereas assigning a high rolling dice (like the 
D12) represents leaning it towards its Yang side. And of course the D8 is in 
the middle of the two, representing the most balanced of their five attributes.

CHARACTER DESCRIPTION
---------------------
Players should write a short Description of their character's appearance, for 
future reference.

Characters should have Motivations: what drives them? What are their goals? 
What are they chasing?

Conversely, player's should also think up at least one or two Fears their 
character has. What are they worried about? What makes them anxious? Do they 
perhaps have a phobia, or something that grosses them out?

Players are encouraged to note down a couple Quirks for their character. What 
are random interesting facts about their character that make them unique?

CAPABILITIES
------------
To individuate further, new Characters are assigned 2 Capabilities. A 
Capability represents a non-consumable and specific type of skill, item, or 
defining feature they possess. Examples would include something like singing, 
magic armor, a special power, or perhaps a high status lineage they descend 
from.

One of the capabilities must be Yin, and the other Yang. This represents which 
direction of Duality the capability leans.

ALIGNMENT
---------
Finally, characters are given an Alignment of either -1 (Yin), 0 (Balanced), 
or +1 (Yang). This represents their overall Duality they bias towards, 
impacting all attempts at tasks they perform. A character with a -1 (Yin) 
Alignment has a bias towards calm, collected, and easy going nature, whereas 
a character with a +1 (Yang) alignment will be overall biased towards 
energetic, high emotion, and strong feelings.

CALAMITIES
----------
At character creation players should write down 2-3 Calamities they think 
would be fitting for their character's story that they are comfortable with 
running the risk of happening. A Calamity is a deeply negative event that 
occurs to the Character and alters their behavior and completely changes their 
course in life (see "Leave it to Fate" section for details).


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ATTEMPTING TASKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Whenever a Player wishes for their Character to perform a task that may not 
necessarily succeed, they must pass a few checks to validate success.

To begin, the Attribute and its Duality are determined, based on how the 
player expresses their character will perform the task. For example, if they 
attempt to pick a lock with a lockpick (a fine motor skill involving 
dexterity), that would be a Yin Alacrity task they are attempting.

The Player may then choose one of three ways to attempt the task. They can 
Spend their Dice, use their Passive Score, or Leave it to Fate (see section 
"Leave it to Fate").

SPENDING DICE
-------------
Spending their Dice involves rolling the associated Dice with their Attribute. 
The result of this roll constitutes their Base Score. Once they have spent a 
Dice, they cannot spend it again until it is Recharged (see the section 
"Recharging Dice").

PASSIVE SCORE
-------------
If they wish to use their Passive Score instead of Spending their Dice (or 
they no longer have the dice to spend), they will look up the "Passive Score" 
value on their character sheet and use that as their Base Score instead.

The Passive Scores are:
  D4  = 1
  D6  = 4
  D8  = 5
  D10 = 5
  D12 = 6

APPLYING BONUSES
----------------
Whether they choose to Spend their Dice or use their Passive Score, everyone 
at the table will collectively agree if any of the Character's Capabilities 
are reasonably applicable to this Task, for better or for worse. Only one 
Capability can be selected. If multiple are applicable, players should come to 
a reasonable conclusion about which is the most applicable. A Yin capability 
confers a -1 applied to their Base Score, whereas a Yang skill adds a +1 
instead. The Character's Alignment is added to the score in the same way.

DETERMINING SUCCESS
-------------------
The final value of this result is their Final Score, and it must be 2 or less 
to succeed at a Yin task, or a 7 or greater to succeed at a Yang task.


EXAMPLE: ATTEMPTING A TASK
---------------------------
Alex: Alright, I think I'm going to have my character, Jerome, attempt to 
pick the lock on the treasure chest.

Charlie (Mediator): Alrighty, well I think Lockpicking very much sounds like 
a fine motor skill that takes patience and dexterity, so lets call that a Yin 
Alacrity roll.

Alex: For sure, I still have my Alacrity dice and its a D4, so I think it's 
high time I spend it.

Riley: Hey, didn't you also give Jerome Metallurgy as their Yin skill? I 
think it's reasonable that it would apply to lockpicking, lots of lockpicks 
start out as blacksmiths.

Charlie: I agree, I think that can apply.

Alex: Okay cool, Jerome has a Yang alignment so that'll cancel that out for 
this case, leaving me with a neutral +0 bonus. Should be a 50/50 chance I 
suceed.

<Alex Rolls a 2 on their D4>

Charlie: Nice, you successfully are able to apply your metallurgy knowledge 
and pick the lock, the treasure chest pops open!


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEAVE IT TO FATE (LUCK ROLLS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Alternatively, a Player can choose to Leave it to Fate by rolling with Luck, 
which always uses the D20.

Luck rolls do not gain any Yin nor Yang bonuses. Failure is determined by 
rolling a 10 or less, and success an 11 or higher. It is purely a 50/50 chance 
of failure/success.

Note that any Luck related tasks (like gambling or anything else that is truly 
up to chance) should only use a Luck Roll instead of any of the five 
Attributes, as there is no skill involved in such tasks.

LUCK ROLL RESULTS
-----------------
The D20 Luck roll has the following outcomes:

  1       = CALAMITY (Burn a Calamity, Shift your Attributes)
  2-5     = CRITICAL FAILURE (Additional Penalty)
  6-10    = FAILURE
  11-15   = SUCCESS
  16-19   = CRITICAL SUCCESS (Extra Bonus)
  20      = MIRACLE (Progress your character to a new Milestone)

If the player uses a Luck Roll as a substitute for an actual Yin or Yang 
related task, they run the risk of a Critical Failure (Rolling a 2 to 5) or 
worse, a Calamity can befall them (Rolling a 1).

Inversely though, they can achieve a Critical Success (Rolling 16 to 19), or 
a Miracle (Rolling 20).

CRITICAL FAILURES
-----------------
Critical Failures incur an additional penalty, typically some form of small 
penalizing downside to the environment or character. An example would be 
breaking one's tool when trying to perform a craft.

CALAMITIES
----------
Worse yet is a Calamity, which is a deeply negative event that occurs to the 
Character. It alters their behavior and completely changes their course in 
life. When a Calamity occurs, the Player must look at their character sheet 
and shift all their attributes either 1 slot up or 1 slot down, representing 
a disruption to their characters balance and duality. For example if they 
choose to shift slots up, their D4 Attribute would become their D6, their D6 
becomes their D8, and so on. Furthermore their D12 Attribute would "wrap 
around" back to the bottom, becoming their new D4.

Finally, a character that has had a calamity befall them is Out of Luck, and 
cannot use Luck Rolls for the rest of the session.

CRITICAL SUCCESSES
------------------
A Critical Success grants an additional minor boon that may occur, perhaps an 
additional clue to the story they uncover, or completing the task much faster 
than usual.

MIRACLES
--------
Conversely, if the Luck roll achieves a Miracle (a roll of exactly 20), the 
character receives some form of critical advancement in their personal 
storyline. This should relate to their Motivations in some manner. Whatever 
is chosen, it should be a substantial Milestone for their character's progress 
towards their personal goals. As an additional bonus, the player may choose to 
optionally Shift their character stats up or down in the same manner as a 
Calamity, if they wish.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONSUMABLES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Sometimes, during the story (especially as a reward for great efforts), 
characters will receive some form of Consumable. A consumable represents an 
effect (much like a Capability) they can utilize to gain a needed extra bonus 
on one of their attempts at tasks.

Typically Consumables are one time use, and once Consumed they cannot be used 
again. However this is not a mandatory restriction and Mediators are welcome 
to come up with clever and interesting Consumables for players to enjoy. An 
example of a more interesting restriction could perhaps be a Consumable that 
can be used three times, but with some form of downside that must be carefully 
considered before using.

Examples could include: A magic potion, a favor from someone they can call in, 
or perhaps a bit of gained reputation they can lean on (but not abuse).

WHEN TO USE CONSUMABLES
-----------------------
Players have two opportunities during Attempting a Task in which they can 
decide if they wish to Consume a Consumable to gain its bonus. Both only apply 
to when they are Attempting a Yin or Yang Task; Consumables cannot be used on 
Luck rolls.

The first opportunity is before they Spend a Dice. If they spend their 
Consumable before the dice roll, they can choose to pre-emptively gain a -2 
Yin Bonus or +2 Yang Bonus, effectively double its normal bonus.

The second opportunity is after they have Spent a Dice or used their Passive 
Score for Attempting a Task, and have finished calculating their Final Score. 
If they still choose to use a Consumable at this stage to get a last bit of 
extra boost to turn a Failure into a Success, they may only get a -1 Yin bonus 
or +1 Yang bonus at this stage.

ALTERNATIVE CONSUMABLES
-----------------------
Mediators may also choose to grant alternative Consumables that can 
conditionally Recharge dice or perform other interesting effects, like letting 
a player substitute one of their Attributes for another for Attempting a Task. 
Feel free to get really creative!


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RECHARGING DICE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Whenever an effect causes players to Recharge Dice, the respective spent dice 
are returned back to them, allowing the player to spend them once again.

At the beginning of a Session, all player's dice are Recharged.

Whenever all the dice of every Player's Characters in a session have been 
collectively spent, everyone recharges all their dice. This should be 
represented narratively as everyone taking some form of short break, or 
perhaps finding somewhere to rest. When everyone's quantities of remaining 
dice are beginning to dwindle, this should signal to the players to begin 
leaning the narrative towards some form of time of rest for everyone. Perhaps 
finding a local Inn or Tavern to rest at, or finding somewhere to set up camp.

Players with a remaining dice can choose to spend it on a solitary final task 
in order to satisfy the conditions to recharge everyone's dice. Examples could 
include researching something important, studying, or perhaps preparing for 
the next day.

Note that though the above examples can be used as a way to "burn" up leftover 
dice for a player who has many leftover, it is recommended that players avoid 
using this as a means to enable certain players to hog the spotlight too much 
in the narrative, then have other's use their numerous leftover dice on 
solitary tasks instead of participating. A balanced group with an observant 
Mediator should try and make sure everyone feels included, and a player with 
many extra dice is typically an indicator that the spotlight is not being 
shared equally.

An excellent benchmark to roughly aim for is every player to have 1 dice 
leftover after the proverbial dust has settled and it is time for rest, 
allowing everyone to have some form of final solitary narrative event before 
recharging.


EXAMPLE: RECHARGING DICE
-------------------------
Charlie (Mediator): Alright, how are we all doing on our dice pools?

Avery: That was my last one I just spent, so my character is pretty beat, 
"I think it's about time we find somewhere to set up camp" they comment to 
the group.

Riley: I have just have on left, my D8, so my character agrees with Avery's, 
it's getting pretty late.

Alex: Yeah I also just have my D6 left, so I think Jerome starts keeping an 
eye out for a good spot.

Charlie: Alright! So you in fact spot a pretty decent little grove just off 
trail, looks like the perfect spot to hunker down for the night.

Riley: Okay cool, well since I may as well, is it fine if my character spends 
their last D8 further studying that spell they've been investigating the past 
few nights?

Charlie: For sure, let's have that as a Yang roll as usual.

Alex: I think Jerome is going to do some light training with their last bit 
of energy for the night, to try and improve their techniques.

Charlie: No problem, lets give that a Yang roll as well.

Riley: Oh hey! I finally passed the check!

Alex: Me too!

Charlie: Awesome, you both finally make progress on your work that night, we 
can get to that in a second. Also, everyone's pools are spent now so you all 
get a good nights rest and recharge your dice pools!
